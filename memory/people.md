## Audience

**Who:** SQL practitioners who already use the language at work — analysts, data engineers, BI developers — but encounter edge cases and pattern failures they cannot always explain.

**What they need:** Not introductions to SQL. Explanations of *why* one approach fails while another works. The hook works specifically because it names a trap an experienced person could fall into without knowing it. "ROW_NUMBER silently drops ties" lands for someone who has used window functions but has never hit the edge case.

**What they do not need:** Definition of GROUP BY. Explanation of what a CTE is. Basic JOIN mechanics. Assume competence and address the gap.

**Business frames they respond to:** Content platforms (Yelp, leaderboards), performance management (sales targets), retention analysis (account status), compensation equity — concrete scenarios where the wrong query produces a report that looks correct and misleads a decision.

**Size and nature:** LinkedIn data/analytics community. Primarily SE Asian professionals, given Nivan's network, but SQL patterns are universal so reach extends further.
