Sometimes the easiest way to compute a percentage in SQL is not dividing two separate COUNT functions.

💻 𝗦𝗤𝗟 𝗼𝗳 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝗗𝗮𝘆: Percentage of Shipable Orders (StrataScratch)
🔗 Link in comments

Problem: Find the percentage of shipable orders. Consider an order is shipable if the customer's address is known.

─────────────────────────────

🧠 𝗦𝗤𝗟 𝗦𝗼𝗹𝘂𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻

SELECT
    AVG((address IS NOT NULL)::INT) * 100 AS percent_shipable
FROM orders
INNER JOIN customers
    ON customers.id = orders.cust_id;

─────────────────────────────

🧩 𝗦𝗶𝗺𝗽𝗹𝗲 𝗹𝗼𝗴𝗶𝗰 𝗯𝗿𝗲𝗮𝗸𝗱𝗼𝘄𝗻:
→ JOIN: Connects `orders` with `customers` to check address availability for each order.
→ address IS NOT NULL: Evaluates to a boolean (true or false) based on whether the address is valid.
→ AVG() * 100: Calculates the mean of 1 and 0 values, which mathematically equals dividing the valid address count by the total order count.

─────────────────────────────

📊 𝗧𝗵𝗶𝘀 𝗽𝗮𝘁𝘁𝗲𝗿𝗻 𝘁𝗲𝗹𝗹𝘀 𝘆𝗼𝘂:
• Validating delivery inputs: Establishing logic that checks for a complete address before dispatching shipments is essential.
• Cost reduction: Verifying this upfront will streamline operations and prevent sunk costs from packages that will never arrive.

─────────────────────────────

🎯 𝗞𝗲𝘆 𝘁𝗮𝗸𝗲𝗮𝘄𝗮𝘆𝘀:
1. Pay close attention to exactly what must be calculated. Choosing between COUNT(), SUM(CASE WHEN ...), and AVG() depends entirely on your objective and each approach serves a distinct purpose.
2. The `AVG((condition)::INT)` trick is an elegant shorthand for calculating percentages without dividing two lengthy aggregate expressions.
