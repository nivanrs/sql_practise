A paid order is not always a shippable order.

💻 SQL of the Day: Percentage of Shipable Orders

Problem: Find the percentage of orders that can actually be shipped. An order only counts as shippable when the customer's address is known.

─────────────────────────────

🧠 SQL Solution

SELECT
    100.0
    * COUNT(CASE WHEN c.address IS NOT NULL THEN 1 END)
    / COUNT(*) AS percent_shipable
FROM orders AS o
INNER JOIN customers AS c
    ON o.cust_id = c.id;

─────────────────────────────

🧩 Logic Breakdown
→ Join orders with customers so every order can be checked against the customer's address record.
→ CASE WHEN counts only orders with valid addresses.
→ Divide by COUNT(*) for total orders, multiply by 100.0 for percentage (floating-point division).

Alternative: AVG((address IS NOT NULL)::INT) * 100 is more concise but less explicit.

─────────────────────────────

📊 Business Impact
• Fulfillment readiness: Orders without addresses may look valid in the order table, but operations teams cannot ship them.
• Cost control: Measuring this early helps teams avoid wasted picking, packing, routing, and customer support work.

─────────────────────────────

🎯 Key Takeaways
1. Use COUNT(CASE WHEN ... THEN 1 END) / COUNT(*) for explicit percentage calculations.
2. The 100.0 (not 100) ensures floating-point division, preventing integer truncation.
